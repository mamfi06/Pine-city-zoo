LION

Lions are large, powerful carnivores known as the "king of the jungle," though they actually live in savannas and grasslands across sub-Saharan Africa (and a small population in Indiaâ€™s Gir Forest).

They are the only big cats that live in social groups, called prides, which usually consist of related females, their cubs, and a few dominant males.

Male lions are easily recognized by their thick manes, which vary in color and size and can signal health and strength.

Females (lionesses) do most of the hunting, working together to catch prey such as antelope, zebras, and buffalo.

Lions are apex predators, but they also scavenge and steal kills from other animals.

Lions rest for up to 20 hours a day, becoming active mainly in the evening and early morning.

They communicate through roars, scent marking, facial expressions, and body language.

A lions roar can be heard up to 5 miles away and is used to warn rivals and locate pride members.

Lions face threats from habitat loss, human conflict, and poaching, and their populations are in decline in the wild.
