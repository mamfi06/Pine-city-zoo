WARTHOG

Warthogs are wild members of the pig family found in the grasslands, savannas, and woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa.

They are easily recognized by their large heads, curved tusks, and warty bumps on their faces, which are actually thick growths of skin used for protection during fights.

Both males and females have tusks, but the males are usually larger and more curved.

Warthogs are herbivores, feeding mainly on grasses, roots, berries, and bark, though they will sometimes eat insects or carrion.

They often kneel on their front legs while grazing, using tough pads on their knees. Warthogs live in burrows, often taking over abandoned aardvark holes.
