ANIMALS MAPS PLACES

MONKEY

Monkeys are highly intelligent and social animals that belong to the primate order. They are divided into two main groups: Old World monkeys, found in Africa and Asia (like baboons and macaques), and New World monkeys, found in Central and South America (like capuchins and howler monkeys).

Most monkeys live in tropical forests, though some, like baboons, live in savannas or mountainous areas. They are generally arboreal (tree-dwelling), but some species spend much of their time on the ground.

Monkeys have grasping hands and feet with opposable thumbs, making them excellent climbers. Many New World monkeys have prehensile tails, which act like an extra limb for gripping branches.

They are omnivorous, eating fruits, leaves, seeds, insects, and sometimes small animals or bird eggs. Monkeys are very social, living in groups called troops that can range from a few individuals to over a hundred, depending on the species.

They communicate using a variety of vocalizations, facial expressions, and body language, and they often form strong social bonds through grooming and play.
