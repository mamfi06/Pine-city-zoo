GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest primates on Earth and are known for their strength, intelligence, and close genetic relationship to humansâ€”they share about 98% of their DNA with us.

Native to the forests of Central Africa, gorillas live in two main species: the eastern gorilla and the western gorilla, each with its own subspecies, such as the mountain gorilla and lowland gorilla.

Gorillas are primarily herbivores, feeding on leaves, stems, fruit, and bamboo. They live in social groups called troops or bands, usually led by a dominant silverback male, named for the patch of silver hair on his back.

Silverbacks protect the group, make decisions, and settle disputes. Despite their size and power, gorillas are generally gentle and peaceful, only showing aggression when threatened.

They are mostly ground-dwelling and move around by knuckle-walking. Gorillas build nests out of leaves and branches each night to sleep in, and they rarely drink water directly because their plant-based diet provides most of the moisture they need.

Gorillas are highly intelligent, capable of using tools, solving problems, and even learning elements of sign language in captivity.
